#TalkTrashNewYork

#TalkTrashNewYork is DSNY's call to action, encouraging New Yorker to do their share by putting litter where it goes - in a basket.

(Reminding fellow New Yorker to do the same helps, too!)

Don't trash New York

  • Litter trashes our town. Garbage along our streets and sidewalks
  • reaches our sewers, then floats to our beaches - where it hurs
  • wildlife, damages our landscape and messes up summer.

  • To keep New York City beautiful and protect our environment, the
  • NYC Department of Sanitation empties more than 23,500
  • sidewalks across the five boroughs and mechanical brooms
  • sweep more than XXX miles of streets and highway ramps each
  • week.

Here's Why

  • Littering can be hazardous to one's own health as well. Trash in an
  • area attracts vermin and bacteria. Broken glass and other sharp
  • objects are dangerous when they are left lying around in public
  • places.
  • Litter is harmful to wildlife as well. Plastic can cause animals such
  • as birds to choke, when they mistake it for food. And, even worse,
  • small bits of plastic can accumulate in the stomach of a bird,
  • never digesting or decomposing, to the point where there is no
  • more room for real food and the bird starves to death with a full
  • stomach.
  • Lastly, but most obviously, litter makes an area look bad. When a
  • large amount of litter can be found in a particular environment, it
  • reflects badly on the people who live there. [http://
  • environment.newarchaeology.com/littering.php]
